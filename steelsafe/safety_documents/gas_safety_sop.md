# Coke Oven Battery Gas Safety Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

## SECTION A: Gas Sensor Thresholds and Action Levels
Gas safety monitoring relies on real-time alerts. Two tiers of action levels are defined:
1. Carbon Monoxide (CO):
   - Action Level (Secondary Threshold): 35 ppm. Demands investigation.
   - High Warning (Traditional Alarm): 100 ppm. Warrants work suspension.
   - Critical Level: 200 ppm. Mandates immediate evacuation.
2. Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S):
   - Action Level (Secondary Threshold): 5 ppm. Demands investigation.
   - High Warning (Traditional Alarm): 10 ppm. Warrants work suspension.
   - Critical Level: 200 ppm. Mandates immediate evacuation.

## SECTION B: Olfactory Fatigue (H2S Odor Fatigue)
Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S) has a characteristic "rotten egg" smell at low concentrations.
However, at concentrations exceeding 10 ppm, H2S causes rapid olfactory fatigue (odor fatigue), paralyzing the worker's sense of smell.
Workers will cease to smell the gas and may falsely assume the threat has cleared. This is a critical trap: workers must never rely on their sense of smell for H2S detection. Evacuation and standby safety teams must monitor electronic sensors.

## SECTION C: Gas Collection Main Pressure Drop and Leaks
The Gas Collection Main (GCM) operates under positive pressure (typically 103.2 kPa ± 0.8 kPa) to collect raw coke oven gas.
A sudden pressure drop below the zone baseline by 2.5 kPa or more indicates a potential upstream leak, main crack, or header integrity failure.
If a pressure drop is detected while any permit-to-work (PTW) is active, incoming supervisors must suspend work and clear the GCM catwalk immediately to protect workers from raw gas inhalation.
