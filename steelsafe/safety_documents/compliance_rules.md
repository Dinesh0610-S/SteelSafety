# SteelSafe Compliance and Audit Standards Reference

## SECTION 5: Gas Calibration Requirements
OISD Standard 137 Section 5 requires that all fixed and portable gas monitoring detectors (CO, H2S, and LEL) must undergo routine calibration checks every 30 days. Any detector exceeding 30 days without certified recalibration represents a severe compliance deviation (High priority) and must be serviced immediately.

## SECTION 6: Weekly safety audits
DGMS Safety Circular 24 requires that weekly safety audits and PPE inspections must be conducted in all active gas hazard zones (Charging Platform and Coke Oven Battery) every 7 days. This audit must verify compliance with hard hat, safety boot, and breathing apparatus mandates. Failure to conduct this audit within 7 days constitutes a regulatory deviation (Medium priority).

## SECTION 7: Personnel Medical Checks
Under Factories Act Section 41-C, all personnel deployed to hazardous process zones must undergo a Periodic Medical Examination (PME) every 12 months. Deploying a worker whose PME is overdue by more than 365 days is a statutory compliance failure (Medium priority).

## SECTION 8: Standby Observer Mandate
Factories Act Section 36 mandates that any active Confined Space Entry (CSE) permit must have a designated standby observer stationed outside the entry point. The observer must verify communication and breathing apparatus safety. Working inside a confined space without an observer present is an critical safety violation (High priority).
