# OISD Standard 137: Hazardous Work Permit System

## SECTION 1: Hot Work Permit Requirements
A Hot Work Permit is mandatory for any activity involving open flames, sparks, welding, grinding, or any potential ignition source in hazardous areas (including Gas Collection Main and Coke Oven Battery).
Before starting hot work, gas testing must be conducted. The area must be cleared of combustible gases.
If gas concentration trends upward (such as Carbon Monoxide rising trend) or if CO concentration exceeds 35 ppm, all hot work permits in the affected zone must be suspended immediately. Starting welding near a rising gas trend represents a critical hazard that can lead to catastrophic explosion.

## SECTION 2: Confined Space Entry Rules
Entry into confined spaces (like battery flues, regenerators, and gas mains) is strictly regulated. A Confined Space Entry (CSE) permit must be active.
Under no circumstances shall workers enter a confined space if the gas concentration exceeds the action levels (CO >= 35 ppm or H2S >= 5 ppm). Constant gas monitoring must be maintained.
Any Confined Space Entry must have a dedicated standby person stationed outside the entry point to monitor atmospheric conditions and coordinate emergency evacuations.

## SECTION 3: Shift Changeover and Handover Supervision
During shift changeovers, supervision transfers and communication gaps frequently occur. This period (typically ±15 minutes around shift boundaries) represents a high operational risk.
All ongoing hazardous permits must be reviewed and formally handed over between incoming and outgoing shift supervisors.
Incoming supervisors must inspect active permit locations to verify that gas levels remain safe and that no unauthorized worker clusters have formed in high-risk zones. Reduced supervision during changeover boundaries represents a primary causal factor in industrial incidents.
